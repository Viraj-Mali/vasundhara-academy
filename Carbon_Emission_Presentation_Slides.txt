Slide 2: Introduction / Problem Definition
- Traditional emission prediction uses manual calculations and statistical models.
- Existing systems have low accuracy for complex and dynamic datasets.
- Increasing carbon emissions demand accurate prediction models.
- Need for automation, real-time handling, and proper visualization.

---

Slide 3: Abstract
- System uses ML to analyze historical data and predict future emissions.
- Evaluates factors like energy consumption, industrial activity, and transport.
- Uses algorithms like Linear Regression and Random Forest Regression.
- Aids policymakers and organizations in sustainable development.

---

Slide 4: Objectives
- Predict future carbon emission levels accurately.
- Analyze historical trends.
- Automate carbon emission analysis.
- Provide graphical visualization for better decision making.
- Contribute toward climate change mitigation.

---

Slide 5: Scope & Target Users
Scope:
- Predicting and analyzing emission patterns.
- Supporting government and industrial planning.
- Scalable to smart cities and environmental agencies.

Users:
- Government Authorities (formulate policies)
- Industries (optimize energy usage & compliance)

Slide 9: Database Design
- User Table: Manages access (user_id, username, password).
- Emission Data Table: Stores historical records (year, energy used, industrial & transport emissions).
- Prediction Table: Stores forecasted results and the ML models used.

Slide 10: Testing Strategies
- Manual testing for UI Validation.
- Unit Testing for individual modules (Data loading, Preprocessing, ML training).
- Integration Testing for backend-frontend link.
- Acceptance Testing for accuracy verification and report generation.

Slide 11: Advantages
- Automated and Fast.
- High Accuracy via Machine Learning.
- Visual Data Comparison (Charts/Graphs).
- Aids Strategic Planning.

---

Slide 12: Limitations
- Dependent heavily on historical data accuracy.
- Lacks real-time data continuous integration.
- Cannot predict sudden events (natural disasters, pandemics).

---

Slide 13: Proposed Enhancements

- Use of Deep Learning (LSTM) for time-series forecasting.
- Cloud Deployment for scalability.
- Mobile App support for better accessibility.
